//What is Pearl?

Pearl (PRL) is a Layer-1 blockchain with an unusual heart: instead of burning electricity on throwaway hashes, it secures the chain with proof-of-useful-work. Miners run large noisy matrix multiplications — the exact operation behind AI training, inference and fine-tuning — hash the result into a commitment, and attach a zero-knowledge proof so the rest of the network can verify the work cheaply. The team calls this NoisyGEMM, and it runs with almost no overhead versus a plain matrix multiply.

In plain terms: the work your GPU does to mine PRL is the same kind of work that powers modern AI. Pearl launched its mainnet on 27 April 2026, was built by Pearl Research Labs on a peer-reviewed cryptography paper, has a fixed maximum supply of 2.1 billion PRL, and targets a new block roughly every three minutes. PRL trades on SafeTrade.

//What a pool does

Finding a block on your own is a lottery — you might wait weeks for a lump sum, or never hit one. A pool fixes that. Everyone points their miners at the same place, the pool combines all that work, and whenever anyone in the pool finds a block, the reward is shared out by contribution. You trade the occasional jackpot for steady, predictable payouts.

Pearler Pool is pooled-only — no solo mining. You submit shares (proofs that your GPU is doing real work), and your slice of each block is proportional to the shares you contributed.

//How rewards are split — PPLNS

We use PPLNS (Pay-Per-Last-N-Shares). When the pool finds a block, it looks back over the most recent window of shares and pays each miner in proportion to what they contributed in that window. It rewards consistent mining, doesn't over-reward people who hop in and out, and keeps the pool's incentives honest. A flat 1% pool fee keeps the lights on; everything else is yours.
